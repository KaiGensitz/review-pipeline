    2) Urban Context: The type of urban setting and how the built environment, city, metropolitan area, location, or real-world environment is defined.
    3) Physical Activity Outcomes: Primary or planned PA outcome measured, such as MVPA, step count, walking, active transport, sedentary time, exercise adherence, or intervention-related PA behavior.
    4) RQ1 (AI & Technology):
        - AI Architecture: Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Generative AI/LLM, Reinforcement Learning/Bandits, or Rule-Based.
        - Computing Architecture: On-device/Edge AI, Cloud-based, or Hybrid.
        - PA Role: Assessment (sensing/monitoring), Promotion (intervention/JITAI), or Both.
        - Smartphone Delivery: Native App, Web-App, SMS/Chatbot, or Wearable-linked App.
        - Sensing Modalities: GPS, Accelerometer, Camera, Text/Log, wearable sensors, or self-report.
    5) RQ2 (Psychosocial Integration):
        - Explicit Theory: e.g., Social Cognitive Theory, Transtheoretical Model, Self-Determination Theory, COM-B, or other named behavioral theory.
        - Behavior Change Techniques (BCTs): e.g., goal setting, feedback, prompts/cues, action planning, self-monitoring, social support, rewards, tailoring, or coaching.
    6) RQ3 (Inclusivity & Ethics): Specific target populations such as older adults, chronic disease groups, underserved communities, sex/gender groups, accessibility needs, and ethical considerations such as privacy, GDPR, consent, bias mitigation, fairness, explainability, and data security.
    7) RQ4 (Sustainability): Green AI, energy-efficient computation, battery optimization, low-power sensing, model efficiency, active transport, green exercise, nature exposure, or other pro-environment behavior promotion.
    8) Synthesis: Key findings, implementation implications, study limitations, and any reviewer notes needed to interpret ambiguous extraction decisions.
