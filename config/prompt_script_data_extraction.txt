1) Critical requirement ("Quote-and-Code"): Use ONLY the provided evidence bundle: the primary PDF text and, when present, user-supplied Supplemental Cited Evidence blocks. Read table-like text carefully and use the nearest row/column label in the quote when the PDF text extraction preserves it. For fields named overall, prefer full-cohort or overall baseline summaries over arm-specific, site-specific, completer-only, or follow-up subgroup values. If age is reported as categories or bands, preserve the categories exactly with counts/percentages; do not compute a mean from category midpoints. If tables report separate groups or clearly labeled participant subsets, capture values with labels and compute an overall weighted mean only when actual group means and group sizes are explicitly reported. For protocols without enrolled baseline values, extract eligibility age ranges or thresholds when the schema permits this fallback.

6) Protocol and Design-Paper Rule: If a protocol, feasibility, usability, field-study, or design paper has no final effectiveness results, extract planned assessments, preliminary status, design findings, feasibility/usability results, and stated implications when the schema variable allows them. Do not mark these fields unavailable simply because the paper is not a completed RCT.
7) Context Rule: For country, continent, and setting, prioritize participant recruitment, deployment, clinical site, trial site, app/server deployment, or field-study location. 8) Publication Source Rule: For any field named evidence_source, extract the literature/source type that legitimizes the paper, not its methods section. For smartphone_usage, capture all mobile delivery channels and arms, including native apps, companion apps, SMS/text messaging, push notifications, chatbots, voice-assistant companion apps, wearable synchronization, and data transmission.
11) Synthesis Completeness Rule: For implications and limitations, search discussion, conclusion, design recommendations, limitations, and future-work sections. Preserve concrete author recommendations or caveats such as gamification, persuasive strategies, personalization, multimodal interaction, adaptive feedback, cultural diversity, music personalization, platform restrictions, short duration, small sample, nonresponse, generalizability, and technology constraints.
12) Detail Rule: For auditable fields, prefer concise manuscript labels and preserve reviewer-like keywords over broad synthesis. Keep values close to the paper wording, including delivery channel, interface, setting, geography, age table labels, outcome-domain terms, development-process labels, and Not Available when that is the reviewer-facing extraction. Use longer prose only when the manuscript itself requires it.

# STEPS
Fields to extract (Conceptual Framework):
    1) Study Metadata and Population: First author/year, country, sample size, study design, age, gender, ethnicity, health status, duration, timepoints, funding, and conflicts of interest. Search abstracts, methods sections, participant/inclusion criteria, baseline characteristics tables, participant flow descriptions, and trial registration summaries before using missing-value defaults.
    2) Urban Context: The type of urban setting and how the built environment, city, metropolitan area, location, clinic, community, home, workplace, field-study location, recruitment site, deployment location, or real-world environment is defined.
    3) Physical Activity Outcomes: Primary, planned, feasibility/usability, or behavior-change PA outcome measured, such as MVPA, step count, walking, active transport, sedentary time, exercise adherence, intervention engagement, intervention-related PA behavior, or planned sensor/questionnaire assessments.
    4) RQ1 (AI & Technology):
        - AI Architecture: Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Generative AI/LLM, Reinforcement Learning/Bandits, or Rule-Based.
        - Computing Architecture: On-device/Edge AI, Cloud-based, or Hybrid.
        - PA Role: Assessment (sensing/monitoring), Promotion (intervention/JITAI), or Both.
        - Smartphone Delivery: Native App, Web-App, SMS/Chatbot, or Wearable-linked App.
        - Sensing Modalities: GPS, Accelerometer, Camera, Text/Log, wearable sensors, or self-report.
    5) RQ2 (Psychosocial Integration):
        - Explicit Theory: e.g., Social Cognitive Theory, Transtheoretical Model, Self-Determination Theory, COM-B, or other named behavioral theory.
        - Behavior Change Techniques (BCTs): e.g., goal setting, feedback, prompts/cues, action planning, self-monitoring, social support, rewards, tailoring, or coaching.
    6) RQ3 (Inclusivity & Ethics): Specific target populations such as older adults, chronic disease groups, underserved communities, sex/gender groups, accessibility needs, and ethical considerations such as privacy, GDPR, consent, bias mitigation, fairness, explainability, and data security.
    7) RQ4 (Sustainability): Green AI, energy-efficient computation, battery optimization, low-power sensing, model efficiency, active transport, green exercise, nature exposure, or other pro-environment behavior promotion.
    8) Synthesis: Key findings, design findings, protocol contribution, implementation implications, study limitations, and any reviewer notes needed to interpret ambiguous extraction decisions.
